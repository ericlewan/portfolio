Remoter.me  ·  2023

Website design
built to scale

Designing the public face of a remote work platform — starting from a single logo and building a visual language that extended well beyond the page.

Role

Product Designer

Timeline

2023

Company

Remoter.me

The problem

Remoter.me had a logo. Everything else — type, colour system, spacing, component patterns, visual tone — needed to be built from scratch. The brief was a landing page, but a landing page is also a brand statement. Whatever got designed here would set the visual direction for everything that came after.

The challenge wasn't just designing a page. It was developing a design language that could hold up at scale, using a single existing mark as the only starting point.

The approach

I worked outward from the logo — reading its weight, geometry, and implied personality, then extending those signals into a full visual system. Typeface and colour choices had to feel native to the mark. Spacing and component patterns had to be systematic enough to replicate without redesigning from scratch each time.

The visual direction went dark — a deliberate departure from the lightweight pastels dominating productivity tools at the time. It made the product screenshots feel elevated rather than generic, and gave the brand a distinct presence in a crowded category.

Components were built as a reusable system rather than one-off page elements. That decision paid off quickly — when new pages and social media templates were needed, the visual language was already defined and ready to extend.

The result

A landing page that became a design system. The visual language built around the original logo scaled directly into new website pages, social media assets, and marketing materials — without starting over each time.

The brief was one page. The deliverable was a foundation.
