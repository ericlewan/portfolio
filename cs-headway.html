The context

Headway is TIME's #4 EdTech company and the world's most downloaded book summary app — 150M+ downloads, users across 30+ countries, App Store Editors' Choice. I joined the Web Growth team to design the flows that convert visitors into subscribers.

At this scale, every design decision is measurable. There's no room for intuition alone. What you ship to millions has to be validated — and what works in one market can fail in another.

The challenge

Growth design at scale means you're never designing for one person. You're designing for millions simultaneously — across cultures, devices, price sensitivities, and motivations. The challenge isn't making something look good. It's making something that works across all of them.

Assumptions that seem universal often aren't. Trust signals that resonate with American users fall flat in Germany. Progress language that motivates English speakers needs different framing in French. The same button color, the same copy, the same layout — different outcomes in different markets.

The approach

Everything is a hypothesis. Every design decision starts with a behavioral question: what psychological mechanism are we activating? What friction are we removing? Then we test it.

Progress psychology. Payment method friction. Trust signal placement. Discount sequencing. Each experiment is designed to isolate one variable — and the results constantly challenge what seemed obvious. The best outcomes come from tests I was most skeptical about.

I also work at the intersection of design and copy — the two are inseparable in conversion flows. The right word in the right place changes behavior more than visual polish ever will.

The result

Ongoing. This is the work — a continuous cycle of hypotheses, tests, learnings, and new hypotheses. The compounding effect of systematic testing at scale is the product getting measurably better every sprint.

Still ongoing. The main thing this kind of work teaches: conviction without data is just a guess.
